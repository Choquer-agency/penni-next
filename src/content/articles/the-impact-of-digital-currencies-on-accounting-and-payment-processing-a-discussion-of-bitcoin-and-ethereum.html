Introduction to Bitcoin

‍

A decentralised digital money called Bitcoin was invented in 2009. It was the first cryptocurrency ever created and is frequently referred to as the "gold standard" of virtual money. A peer-to-peer network serves as the foundation for Bitcoin, which is decentralised. As soon as a transaction is confirmed, miners log it on the blockchain, a widely used open ledger.

‍

Benefits of Bitcoin

‍

Bitcoin has a number of benefits over conventional currencies. It is quick, safe, and universal. There are no fees or delays attached to transactions, and they are executed practically immediately. Bitcoin is safe because it protects transactions with encryption. It can also be utilised everywhere in the world because it is worldwide.

‍

The drawbacks of Bitcoin

‍

Using Bitcoin has significant downsides despite its benefits. Its value might change fast and dramatically due to its high volatility. It is not backed by any government or central bank and is not widely recognised as a form of payment. Finally, because it is unregulated, users are not protected if something goes wrong.

Introduction to Ethereum

‍

Ethereum is a blockchain-based decentralised network. The second-largest cryptocurrency in the world, it was established in 2015. Decentralized applications and smart contracts are built and operated on Ethereum. Additionally, it is employed to make transactions and payments easier.
