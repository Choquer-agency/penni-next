several types of data privacy laws

Data privacy rules prevent the unauthorised use of people's personal information. The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the California Consumer Privacy Act are the two most prevalent data privacy laws (CCPA). No matter where the organisation is headquartered, if it handles the personal data of European individuals, it must comply with the GDPR. Any business that gathers or handles the personal data of California residents is subject to the CCPA.

In order to make sure that customer data is not stored for longer than necessary, accounting companies must also create data retention rules. Policies for data retention should outline how long to keep customer data and how to get rid of it when it's no longer required.

Even with the best secure systems, data breaches can still happen. Accounting businesses must to have a plan in place for dealing with data breaches, which should include alerting clients who may be affected and taking action to lessen the breach.

Reporting Infractions of Data Privacy

There should be a procedure in place for reporting data privacy abuses at accounting companies. This entails notifying the appropriate authorities of any violations and taking action to resolve the issue.
