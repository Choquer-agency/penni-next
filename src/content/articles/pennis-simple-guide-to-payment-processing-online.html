Payment Processing Online Explained

‍

Payment processing online is the process by which money moves from a customer's bank account to yours after the customer purchases goods/services online. This process is called an online payment system and consists of 3 major players:

‍

Payment gateway

‍

Ensures that the transaction is completed quickly and securely. This component is essentially a “middleman” between the payment processor and the credit card/ debit card company

  

‍

Payment processor

‍

A third party (like Penni Payments) that communicates the card details between your bank account and the customer’s bank account. The payment goes through if the customer has enough funds in their account.

‍

Merchant account 

‍

At the end of this process, the funds are transferred to your business’ bank account safely and securely.

Getting Started 

‍

Now that you’re familiar with the terms, let’s dive into implementing payment processing online for your business. To do this, you have 2 options: you can either do it all yourself, or outsource to a third-party like Penni Payments.

‍

If you choose to set up the system yourself, you’ll need to create an online payment system that meets Payment Card Industry Data Security Standards (PCI DSS). The PCI DSS checklist ensures that cardholder information is securely transmitted, stored, and handled. Depending on the size of your business, PCI DSS has different requirements you need to meet. 

‍

If you want to start payment processing online, you need to comply with these standards. 

‍
