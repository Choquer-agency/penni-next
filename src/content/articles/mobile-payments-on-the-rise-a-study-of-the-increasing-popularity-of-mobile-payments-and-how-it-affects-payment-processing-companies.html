Mobile Payment Types

‍

There are various different kinds of mobile payments that are accessible. These include in-app purchases, NFC (Near Field Communication) purchases, QR code purchases, and mobile wallet purchases. NFC payments are made by just tapping a device against a payment terminal. They are contactless payments. By scanning a QR code with a smartphone or other device, one can make payments using QR codes. Using a digital wallet like Apple Pay or Google Pay, mobile wallet payments are made. Using a mobile device to purchase products and services inside an app is known as making an in-app purchase.

‍

Mobile Payment Security

‍

Mobile payment security is of the utmost significance. Payment processing businesses are required to follow the Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard in order to protect the security of consumers (PCI DSS). According to this standard, businesses must keep their networks secure, safeguard cardholder data, maintain a vulnerability management programme, have robust access control mechanisms in place, frequently test and monitor their networks, and keep an information security policy. To protect cardholder data, many payment processing businesses also employ tokenization, encryption, and other security procedures.

‍

Compliance with Regulations

‍

Payment processing businesses must abide by additional rules and compliance criteria in addition to the PCI DSS. These include the Know Your Customer (KYC) rules, the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), and the Payment Services Directive 2 (PSD2). Companies that handle payments must also abide by the rules and laws of the nations in which they conduct business.
