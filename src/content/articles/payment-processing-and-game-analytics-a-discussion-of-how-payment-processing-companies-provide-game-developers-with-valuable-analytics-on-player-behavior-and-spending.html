Different Mobile Payments

‍

There are numerous varieties of mobile payments that are accessible. These include in-app purchases, mobile wallet purchases, QR code purchases, and NFC (Near Field Communication) purchases. NFC payments are accomplished by merely tapping a device to a payment terminal and are hence contactless. By using a smartphone or other device to scan a QR code, users can make payments online. Payments for mobile wallets are made through a digital wallet, such Apple Pay or Google Pay. By using a mobile device to purchase goods and services inside an app, in-app payments are made.

‍

Payment Security on Mobile Devices

‍

It is crucial that mobile payments are secure. Payment processing businesses must abide by the Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard in order to protect consumer safety (PCI DSS). According to this standard, businesses must maintain a secure network, safeguard cardholder data, run a vulnerability management programme, put in place robust access control mechanisms, test and monitor networks frequently, and have an information security strategy. Tokenization, encryption, and other security measures are also frequently used by payment processing businesses to safeguard cardholder data.

‍

Compliance with Rules

‍

Companies that handle payments must abide by additional laws and compliance requirements in addition to the PCI DSS. These include the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the Know Your Customer (KYC) laws, and the Payment Services Directive 2 (PSD2). The laws and rules of the nations in which payment processing businesses operate must also be followed.
